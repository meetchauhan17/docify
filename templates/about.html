ABOUT_SYSTEM

System Architecture

A deep dive into the neural pipeline that overrides handwriting into structured data.

The AI Pipeline

01

Upload & Preprocess

Your file (PNG, JPG, or multi-page PDF) is received. Images are upscaled to minimum 1600px, auto-contrast adjusted, contrast boosted 1.6×, and sharpened for maximum OCR accuracy.

Auto-contrastUpscale 1600pxSharpening
02

Step 1 — High-Accuracy OCR

Gemini 2.5 Flash receives a specialized prompt to transcribe every word with 100% fidelity — preserving capitalisation, punctuation, symbols, fractions, and subscripts. Each line is tagged with a semantic prefix: HEADING, SUBHEAD, BODY, BULLET, CENTER, UNDERLN, or TABLE.

Gemini 2.5 FlashSemantic TaggingTable DetectionMath Symbols
03

Step 2 — Layout & Drawing Detection

A second Gemini call analyses the original image for page margins, line spacing, and all non-text visual regions: diagrams, graphs, sketches, arrows, and brackets — each with a normalised bounding box and description.

Margin DetectionLine SpacingArrow/Bracket IDDrawing BBox
04

Merge & Position

Text elements and drawings are merged into a single ordered flow using Y-position fractions. Simple arrows become Unicode characters (⟶ ↗ ↙). Simple brackets become [ ] or { }. Complex drawings are cropped and embedded inline.

Y-Position MergeUnicode ArrowsInline Images
05

Generate Output

The final document is built: Word (.docx) using python-docx with per-paragraph formatting, or PDF using ReportLab with native text rendering. Tables, images, and drawings are embedded at their correct positions. You get an instant download.

python-docxReportLabNative TablesInstant Download

NEURAL_DETECTION_CAPABILITIES

Font Size

Headings, subheadings, and body text each get accurately sized based on their visual prominence.

Line Spacing

Tight (1.0×), normal (1.15×), airy (1.5×), or double-spaced (2.0×) — matched to the original.

Indentation

Bullet points, nested lists, and paragraph first-line indents are preserved in the output.

Text Alignment

Left, centered, right, and justified paragraphs are identified and reproduced.

Bold / Italic / Underline

Emphasis markers from the original handwriting are carried into the formatted output.

Page Margins

Document margins are estimated from the original image and applied uniformly.

Tables

Handwritten tables are converted to native editable table structures in Word and PDF.

Drawings & Diagrams

Sketches, graphs, and flowcharts are cropped and embedded at their correct page position.

DATABASE_QUERY // FAQ

You can upload PNG, JPG/JPEG images, or multi-page PDF files. For best results, ensure the image is well-lit, in focus, and taken straight-on (not at an angle).

Docify AI uses Gemini 2.5 Flash — one of the most capable vision models available. Accuracy depends on handwriting legibility. Clear, dark ink on white paper typically achieves 95–99% accuracy. Very light pencil or cramped writing may see slightly lower results.

Uploaded files are temporarily stored on the server only for the duration of processing. The output document is generated and served to you immediately. Conversion history is stored only in your browser's localStorage — never on any server.

Auto mode runs a two-step AI pipeline: first OCR, then layout detection. The AI determines font size, spacing, margins, and alignment from your handwriting. Manual mode lets you specify all formatting parameters yourself — font family, size, line spacing, margins, alignment, and bold/italic/underline.

Yes! Tables are detected and converted into native editable tables in Word and PDF. Diagrams, sketches, and flowcharts are cropped from the original image and embedded inline at the correct position in the output document.

A typical single-page image converts in 15–30 seconds. Multi-page PDFs take proportionally longer — each page is processed individually. On rare occasions, high API load may trigger automatic retries with exponential backoff (2s, 4s, 8s, 16s).
